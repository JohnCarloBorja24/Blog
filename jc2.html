COME TO SCHOOL WITH ME!

May 5, 2025     John Carlo Borja     College Life

Hello, dear readers! Today, I want to give you a glimpse into my daily life as a Filipino college student. Lately, things have been quite stressful and exhausting, but sharing my experiences with you helps me stay grounded—and hopefully, it will resonate with some of you too.

My Morning Routine: Starting Early Despite the Challenges

I usually have afternoon classes starting at 1 PM. Sometimes, I struggle to wake up early because of staying up late either working on assignments or just fixing things around my schedule. But even on those rough nights, I manage to get up around 7 AM.

My mornings are pretty simple, but I want to share this little routine with you: first thing after waking up, I drink a glass of water to refresh myself. Then, I do some light exercises to get energized. After that, I check my group chats for any announcements from my professors or classmates.

Breakfast varies—it depends on my mood and energy. Sometimes I cook at home, but often I buy breakfast from the many street vendors near our place. It's convenient and affordable, especially on days when I feel too tired to cook.

Preparing for School: Small Essentials and Big Decisions

Getting ready for school always takes a bit of mental prep too. I carefully pick my outfit for the day—usually something comfortable because sweating is inevitable under the sun and with all the walking involved.

My usual commute involves a walk to the HBC jeepney stop, which takes me directly to TCU. This walk doubles as part of my exercise, so I don't mind it. In my bag, I keep the essentials: a pen, extra clothes, and handkerchiefs to wipe off sweat. Staying hydrated and bringing a small fan during the hot midday classes is a must!

At School: Learning, Socializing, and Self-Reflection

I often arrive at school before 1 PM, giving me some time to catch up with classmates. Socializing helps me decompress and maintain balance during the hectic semester.

Before class starts, if I'm unsure about the lesson topic, I make it a point to review it quickly so I can participate confidently during recitations. Although I sometimes mix up my thoughts—especially when speaking in English—our professors are always supportive and never put too much pressure on us. This creates a fun and relaxed learning environment.

What I truly appreciate is how our professors encourage us to reflect on ourselves. Their advice often hits home, inspiring me to improve not only academically but also in personal decisions and choices.
